welcome to fountainfinder

our goal

our goal is to create a publicly accessable and editable comprehensive map of both public and proprietary
water sources across the world, so that anyone can find water. Whether this be a student looking for
a source of good water in their school, or a hiker trying to find the nearest spigot to avoid dehydration, or someone living in an urban area
looking for a public faucet.

how to read this map

This map consists of a background map of your building, overlaid with dots corresponding to water sources.
The average quality as recently reported is reflected in the tone of the dot, ranging from green to yellow to red from best to worst.
The "filter level", if the fountain has a bottle filler, will be noted in the color placed in the top right corner of the dot.
Non-functional fountains are represented by an empty grey circle. The floor can be selected with the drop-down in the top left.

how to contribute

leave a quantitave review by ranking the fountain's quality from 0-10 after clicking on it.
you may also add a new fountain if you wish by requesting a trusted representative of the building to add one
with the "contribute" button below the map. If you wish to add your building to our network,
public software to host your personal fountainfinder will be avaliable soon.
